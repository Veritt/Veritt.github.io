What are Roguelike games?

A type of game that can branch off other genres like Action/RPG/FPS that revolves around the player going through procedurally-generated levels, perma-death, unique items/modifiers that can change the way you play and making each run different.

Not every game is the same!

  • Roguelites: The more modern version of roguelikes, usually more action-focused and has meta-progression, where players can unlock permament upgrades to use on the next run. Such games that fall under this is Roboquest, Dead Cells, and Risk of Rain 2.
  • Deck-building Roguelikes: More centered around making builds/decks as powerful as they can be with the player collecting their upgrades through different kinds of cards and perks that have different effects. My favorites would include Slay the Spire, Nubby's Number Factory, Balatro.
  • Tactical Roguelikes: More based around strategic, turn-based combat that the player can plan around by managing units and resources to achieve a goal. Examples include NO-SKIN, Wildermyth, Darkest Dungeon.

How

How do I play Roguelikes?

So it depends on which game you are playing, find one that interests you and just start playing. Most will have a tutorial or just throw you straight into a run, you get a feel for them and will learn about certain mechanics and more meta about the game. What's the best item? How can I optimize my run to be better? Will I find love in my empty life in this eternal suffering of the mortal plane?
